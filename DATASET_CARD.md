# Dataset Card: SPASM DeepSeek-V4-Flash 500 x 10

## Dataset description

SPASM v1.0.0 contains synthetic multi-turn dialogues generated by a
persona-driven Client and a peer Responder. Both conversational roles, the
persona-generation components, and the termination detector used the explicit
DeepSeek-V4-Flash API model in non-thinking mode.

## Size and coverage

- 500 personas
- 10 conversations per persona
- 5,000 conversations
- 44,950 utterances
- 1,371,532 whitespace-tokenized words
- 109 represented interaction domains
- 77 represented occupations
- 50 represented locations
- 12 represented emotional states

All 5,000 records terminated naturally. No record reached the configured cap of
25 turns per agent (50 utterances). Observed conversation lengths range from 4
to 36 utterances, with a mean of 8.99 and a median of 8.

## Generation configuration

- Dataset version: `v1.0.0`
- Requested and returned model: `deepseek-v4-flash`
- Thinking mode: disabled
- Client temperature: 0.7
- Responder temperature: 0.7
- Persona Crafter temperature: 0.7
- Persona Validator temperature: 0.3
- Termination Detector temperature: 0.3
- Maximum turns per agent: 25
- Random seed for structured persona sampling: 20260712
- Candidate pools: 77 occupations, 50 locations, 110 domains, 12 emotions

Generation and rejection sampling used 63,622 successful API requests and
25,189,236 total API-reported tokens. All successful responses used the
`deepseek-v4-flash` model identifier.

## Schema

Each line is a JSON object with:

- `persona_id`: integer from 0 through 499
- `conversation_id`: integer from 0 through 9 within each persona
- `persona_fields`: structured demographic, domain, emotional, and behavioral
  attributes
- `persona_description`: natural-language persona instruction
- `turns`: ordered utterances containing `speaker`, `turn_index`, and `text`
- `termination_reason`: `natural_termination` in v1.0.0

Speakers alternate from `client` to `tested` (Responder), and every conversation
ends with a Responder utterance.

## Validation and post-processing

Automated structural checks confirmed:

- 5,000 valid JSON Lines records
- 500 unique persona IDs and exactly 10 conversations per persona
- No missing or duplicate `(persona_id, conversation_id)` pairs
- No empty messages or invalid turn indices
- Correct alternating speaker order
- No exact duplicate complete conversations
- No explicit AI-identity leakage or hidden-prompt references after QA
- No credential patterns, email addresses, or URLs in the final release

Role QA used full-coverage automated screening with DeepSeek-V4-Flash, followed
by human inspection of flagged records and rejection sampling. The final audit
checkpoint contains 5,000 `none` labels and zero remaining automated flags. This
was not full human annotation and must not be described as such.

Eleven generated strings resembling contact information, account numbers, or
reference IDs were replaced with descriptive placeholders. Counts and
placeholder types are recorded in `metadata/run_stats.json`.

## Intended uses

- Research on persona-conditioned multi-turn dialogue
- Conversation-structure and clustering analysis
- Role-consistency and synthetic-dialogue QA studies
- Exploratory training or evaluation with appropriate downstream safeguards

## Limitations and risks

- Model-generated dialogue can contain inaccurate, unsafe, culturally narrow,
  stereotyped, or overconfident content.
- Medical, legal, financial, mental-health, and safety-related messages are not
  professional advice and have not received expert factual review.
- The persona pool is synthetic and is not representative of real populations.
- Full human annotation was not performed for v1.0.0.
- Automated QA may miss subtle role drift, near-duplicates, factual errors, or
  problematic advice.
- Primarily English generation was intended, but multilingual text can occur.

## Privacy

No real user conversations were collected. Personas and conversations are
synthetic. Contact-like generated strings were redacted to reduce the chance of
accidental coincidence with real contact information.
